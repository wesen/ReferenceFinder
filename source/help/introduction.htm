Welcome to ReferenceFinder!

ReferenceFinder is a tool that helps to construct folding sequences for origami designs that began life as a crease pattern using techniques like box-pleating, tree theory, circle/river theory, or polygon packing. In such designs, the key vertices of the crease are often only defined mathematically and there is no simple way to construct them by folding. ReferenceFinder finds short, efficient, and accurate folding sequences for an arbitrary point (a.k.a. mark) or line on the paper, allowing the origami artist to construct the crease pattern -- or complete model -- by folding alone, with no need to measure and/or mark key points.

The documentation is divided up into several sections. Each section can be printed individually if you'd like to have a hard copy. They are:

  • Background --- gives a little history of this program and its relation to the art of origami.
  • Overview --- gives a short overview, which should be enough to get you started if you don't want to read through the entire documentation.
  • Windows --- describes the major windows of ReferenceFinder and what they're used for.
  • Menus --- describes the menus and their commands.
